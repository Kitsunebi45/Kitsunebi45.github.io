## **The Jurisprudence of Sexual Identity and the Mechanism of But-For Causation**

The legal definition of "sex" has historically been treated as a fixed, biological primitive. However, the United States Supreme Court's decision in *Bostock v. Clayton County*, 140 S. Ct. 1731 (2020), catalyzed an ontological shift by determining that discrimination based on sexual orientation or transgender status is inherently discrimination "because of sex." 1 (Primary; 2020-06-15; US Federal; High reliability; verified). The Court’s logic rests on the "but-for" causation standard: if an employer fires a transgender woman (who was identified as male at birth) for conduct it would tolerate in a cisgender woman, the employer has intentionally penalized the individual for traits or actions it would not have questioned in a member of a different sex. 2 (Secondary/Interpretive; 2021-01-08; US Dept. of Education; Medium reliability/Archived; verified). This effectively collapses the distinction between gender identity and biological sex for the purposes of Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964\. 3 (Primary; 2020-06-15; US Federal; High reliability; verified).  
The dissent in *Bostock*, authored by Justice Alito and joined by Justice Thomas, argued that the majority improperly "legislated" by updating the meaning of "sex" beyond what Congress intended in 1964\. 1 (Primary; 2020-06-15; US Federal; High reliability; verified). This conflict represents a fundamental disagreement in legal ontology: whether the "sex" field is defined by its 1964 "vacuum state" (original public meaning) or by the emergent phenomena of modern social identity. 3 (Primary; 2020-06-15; US Federal; High reliability; verified).

## **The Neurodivergent Field: Disability and the "Record of" Prong**

Neurodivergence, specifically Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), presents a unique case of ontological collapse under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). 31 (Primary; 2025-01-06; US Federal; High reliability; verified). Under 42 U.S.C. § 12102, the definition of disability includes not only an actual impairment but also a "record of such an impairment" or "being regarded as" having one. 31 (Primary; 2025-01-06; US Federal; High reliability; verified).

### **The ADA Amendments Act (ADAAA) and Mitigating Measures**

A central tenet of the ADAAA (2008) is the rejection of the "mitigating measures" doctrine. 33 (Primary; 2008-09-25; US Federal; High reliability; verified). The determination of whether an impairment substantially limits a major life activity (such as concentrating, thinking, or communicating) must be made without regard to medication or "learned behavioral or adaptive neurological modifications." 31 (Primary; 2025-01-06; US Federal; High reliability; verified). In the language of QFT, the underlying "disability field" remains active even if its Level 3 phenomenal output is suppressed through masking or therapy. 13 (Secondary/Theoretical; 2020/2025; Philosophy; Medium reliability; verified).  
The "regarded as" prong is particularly critical for neurodivergent individuals who lack a formal childhood diagnosis. 35 (Secondary/Primary; 2016/2024; US Federal; High reliability; verified). An individual meets the "regarded as" requirement if they establish they were subjected to a prohibited action because of an actual or perceived impairment, regardless of whether that impairment actually limits a major life activity. 31 (Primary; 2016/2025; US Federal; High reliability; verified). This protects those whose "Level 1" virtual traits are detected and stigmatized by others. 12 (Secondary/Theoretical; 2025/2000; Physics/Disability Studies; Medium reliability; verified).
